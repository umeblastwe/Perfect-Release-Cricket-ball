BIOMECHANICS BENCHMARKS
Based on professional cricket bowling standards
🦵 Knee Angle (L & R) ▼
Perfect — Stride leg nearly fully extended at delivery. Maximum energy transfer from run-up to ball. Ideal for fast bowlers maintaining pace and control.
165°–180°
Moderate — Slight bend in the stride leg. Reduces pace but can aid swing or spin bowlers. Acceptable for medium-pace; work on straightening for extra speed.
140°–164°
Needs Work — Significantly bent knee collapses your base. Loss of pace, increased injury risk to knee and back. Focus on front-foot landing drills.
< 140°
💪 Arm Angle at Release ▼
Perfect — High, vertical arm action. Generates steep bounce and maximum pace. Classic fast-bowling form. Think Jasprit Bumrah or Pat Cummins at release.
75°–110°
Moderate — Slightly round-arm or early release. Produces swing and seam movement but less bounce. Good for swing bowlers; acceptable at this level.
45°–74°
Needs Work — Very slinging/round-arm action. High injury risk to shoulder and elbow. Difficult to maintain accuracy. Work with a coach on arm path mechanics.
< 45°
📏 Release Height Score ▼
Perfect — Ball released well above head height relative to ground. Maximum leverage and steep bounce angle. Taller bowlers naturally score higher here.
> 35 pts
Moderate — Decent release point. Some leverage lost, flatter trajectory may result. Try to extend fully through the crease on delivery stride.
20–35 pts
Needs Work — Low release point. Ball arrives flatter and is easier to hit. Work on reaching tall at the crease. Check if front-knee collapse is pulling release point down.
< 20 pts
👟 Stride Count ▼
Optimal — Consistent run-up length. Enough momentum without over-striding and losing control at crease. Most professional fast bowlers use 12–18 strides.
10–20
Short run-up — Less pace generated. Fine for medium-pace or spin bowlers but fast bowlers need more momentum. Try extending run-up gradually to build rhythm.
5–9
Very short or tracking issue — Either the video is a short clip or the AI lost tracking. Check your footage. Ensure full run-up is visible in frame.
0–4
⚡ Run-up Velocity (px/sec) ▼
Fast approach — Good momentum driving into the crease. Translates to higher ball pace and energy. Note: value depends on video resolution and camera distance.
> 300
Moderate approach — Enough momentum for medium-pace. May limit top-end speed for fast bowlers. Work on building controlled acceleration in your run-up.
100–300
Slow approach or static shot — Very limited momentum or camera is too far/close for accurate reading. Ensure bowler moves clearly through the frame.
< 100
