What do your numbers mean?

BIOMECHANICS BENCHMARKS
Based on professional cricket bowling standards
🦵 Knee Angle — L & R (hip → knee → ankle) ▼
Green arc on video — Stride leg nearly fully extended at delivery. Maximum energy transfer from run-up to ball. Ideal for fast bowlers. Both knees should trend toward this zone.
165°–180°
Amber arc on video — Slight bend. Reduces pace but manageable for medium-pace or swing bowlers. Work on extending the front knee to push into the green zone.
140°–164°
White arc on video — Significant knee bend collapses your base. Loss of pace, increased injury risk. Focus on front-foot landing drills and crease stability exercises.
< 140°
💪 Elbow Angle & Extension (shoulder → elbow → wrist) ▼
Elbow Angle ≈ 160°–180° — Arm close to fully straight through delivery. Maximum leverage, steep bounce angle, more pace. Naturally varies by bowler height and action style.
160°–180°
Elbow Ext 0°–15° — The extension delta (how much the arm opens past horizontal). Within normal range for most legal actions. Elbow Ext is the change from the horizontal reference point, not the absolute angle.
0°–15° ext
Elbow Angle < 130° — Notably bent elbow at delivery. Can reduce pace and affect release consistency. Check wrist and shoulder mobility — a tight posterior capsule often causes this.
< 130°
📏 Release Height Score ▼
Ball released well above head height relative to ground. Maximum leverage and steep bounce angle. Taller bowlers naturally score higher — compare against your own baseline over time.
> 35 pts
Decent release point. Some leverage lost; a flatter trajectory may result. Try extending fully through the crease on your delivery stride.
20–35 pts
Low release point — ball arrives flatter and is easier to hit. Check if front-knee collapse is pulling your release point down.
< 20 pts
👟 Stride Count ▼
Consistent run-up length. Enough momentum without over-striding. Most professional fast bowlers use 12–18 strides.
10–20
Short run-up — less pace generated. Fine for medium-pace or spin bowlers. Extend run-up gradually to build rhythm and momentum.
5–9
Very short or tracking gap — either a short clip or AI lost tracking. Check footage length. Ensure the full run-up is visible in frame.
0–4
⚡ Run-up Velocity (px/s) ▼
Strong approach momentum driving into the crease. Value depends on video resolution and camera distance — track relative change session-to-session.
> 300
Moderate approach — enough momentum for medium-pace. May limit top-end speed. Work on controlled acceleration through the last 5–6 strides.
100–300
Slow approach or static shot — very limited momentum or camera position affecting reading. Ensure the bowler moves clearly through the full frame.
< 100
Green — Perfect
Amber — Room to improve
White — Needs work
